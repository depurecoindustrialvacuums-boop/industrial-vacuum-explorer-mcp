# ATEX Zone Classification Guide

This guide explains ATEX zone classifications relevant to industrial vacuum cleaner selection.

## What is ATEX?

ATEX (from French: ATmosphères EXplosibles) refers to two European directives governing equipment used in potentially explosive atmospheres:

- **Directive 2014/34/EU** (ATEX equipment): manufacturer's responsibility
- **Directive 1999/92/EC** (ATEX workplace): employer's responsibility

For dust environments (Type D), zones are classified based on the frequency and duration of explosive dust clouds.

## Dust Zones (per EN 60079-10-2)

| Zone | Definition | Vacuum Category Required |
|------|-----------|-------------------------|
| **Zone 20** | Explosive dust cloud present **continuously**, for long periods, or frequently | Category 1D |
| **Zone 21** | Explosive dust cloud present **occasionally** during normal operation | Category 1D or 2D |
| **Zone 22** | Explosive dust cloud present **rarely and briefly** during normal operation | Category 1D, 2D, or 3D |
| **Ordinary** | No explosive atmosphere expected | No ATEX required (ACD may apply for combustible dust) |

## Gas Zones (per EN 60079-10-1)

| Zone | Definition | Vacuum Category Required |
|------|-----------|-------------------------|
| **Zone 0** | Explosive gas/vapor present continuously | Category 1G |
| **Zone 1** | Explosive gas/vapor present occasionally | Category 1G or 2G |
| **Zone 2** | Explosive gas/vapor present rarely | Category 1G, 2G, or 3G |

## Dust Group Classification (per IEC 60079-0)

Dust groups indicate the conductivity and explosiveness of the dust:

| Group | Dust Type | Examples | Conductivity |
|-------|-----------|----------|--------------|
| **IIIA** | Combustible flyings | Cotton, flax, jute | Non-conductive |
| **IIIB** | Non-conductive dust | Flour, sugar, coal, wood, MDF | Non-conductive |
| **IIIC** | Conductive dust | Aluminum, titanium, magnesium, zinc, copper | Conductive |

**Important:** Equipment certified for Group IIIC can also handle Group IIIA and IIIB dust. All Depureco DEX products are IIIC certified for maximum versatility.

## ACD Classification (Non-ATEX)

ACD (Anti Combustible Dust) is Depureco's classification for vacuums designed for combustible dust in **non-ATEX classified zones**. These products feature:

- Complete grounding system
- Antistatic filter media
- Antistatic accessories
- Spark trap (where applicable)
- Steel construction

**ACD products are NOT certified for use in ATEX zones 21 or 22.** They are designed for environments where combustible dust is present but the area is not classified as explosive (e.g., construction sites with wood dust, woodworking shops, metalworking facilities outside ATEX-classified areas).

## Safety Considerations

### Critical reminders for vacuum selection

1. **Never use a non-ATEX vacuum in an ATEX-classified zone.** This is a serious safety violation and can result in injury, death, and legal liability.

2. **ACD is NOT ATEX certified.** Despite the name, ACD vacuums are for combustible dust in **non-ATEX** environments only. For ATEX zones, choose DEX variants.

3. **Conductive metal dusts require inert liquid bath.** For titanium, aluminum, magnesium powders, the standard DEX vacuum is not sufficient. The INERT variant (with inert liquid bath system) is essential to prevent explosion risk.

4. **Asbestos requires specialized handling.** Depureco can provide guidance, but asbestos work must be performed by certified contractors with proper permits and PPE. Always consult Depureco's technical team for these applications.

5. **Crystalline silica requires Class H filtration.** For construction dust containing silica, EN 60335-2-69 Class H certification is mandatory in many jurisdictions.
