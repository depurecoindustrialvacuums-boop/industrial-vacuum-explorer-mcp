# Filter Class Standards Guide

This guide explains filter class standards for industrial vacuum cleaners according to European and international regulations.

## EN 60335-2-69 Filter Classes

The European standard EN 60335-2-69 (Annex AA) defines three dust classes for industrial vacuum cleaners based on the hazard level of the materials being collected.

### Class L (Low Hazard)

- **Filtration efficiency**: < 1% dust pass-through
- **Use case**: Dust with OEL (Occupational Exposure Limit) > 1 mg/m³
- **Typical materials**: Wood chips, basic construction dust, non-hazardous particles
- **Note**: Not commonly used in Depureco catalog (most products are Class M or higher)

### Class M (Medium Hazard)

- **Filtration efficiency**: < 0.1% dust pass-through
- **Use case**: Dust with OEL between 0.1 and 1 mg/m³
- **Typical materials**:
  - Wood dust (oak, beech)
  - Plaster, cement dust
  - Mineral dust
  - Concrete dust
  - Powder coatings
- **Mandatory requirements**:
  - Filter efficiency test
  - Filter monitoring (acoustic or visual indicator when airflow drops below 20 m/s)
- **Depureco products**: Most standard ACD and DEX variants include Class M filters

### Class H (High Hazard)

- **Filtration efficiency**: < 0.005% dust pass-through (99.995%)
- **Use case**: Carcinogenic, pathogenic, or toxic dust with OEL < 0.1 mg/m³
- **Typical materials**:
  - Crystalline silica (construction, demolition)
  - Lead dust
  - Mold and biological hazards
  - Some pharmaceutical APIs
  - Asbestos (with specialized equipment)
- **Mandatory requirements**:
  - HEPA filter (typically H13 or H14)
  - Sealed dust disposal system
  - Filter integrity test certified
- **Depureco products**: XFLOOR 3H is the dedicated Type H certified product

## EN 1822 HEPA Filter Classification

The European standard EN 1822 classifies absolute (HEPA) filters by their efficiency at the Most Penetrating Particle Size (MPPS).

| Class | Overall Efficiency | Local Efficiency | MPPS Range |
|-------|--------------------|-----------------|------------|
| **E10** | ≥ 85% | — | EPA (not HEPA) |
| **E11** | ≥ 95% | — | EPA (not HEPA) |
| **E12** | ≥ 99.5% | — | EPA (not HEPA) |
| **H13** | ≥ 99.95% | ≥ 99.75% | HEPA |
| **H14** | ≥ 99.995% | ≥ 99.975% | HEPA |
| **U15** | ≥ 99.9995% | ≥ 99.9975% | ULPA |
| **U16** | ≥ 99.99995% | ≥ 99.99975% | ULPA |
| **U17** | ≥ 99.999995% | ≥ 99.9999% | ULPA |

**Depureco standard offering**:
- **H13** — Available as option on most products (99.95% efficiency at 0.3 µm)
- **H14** — Available as upgrade for Class H applications (99.995% efficiency at 0.18 µm)

## Filter Materials

### Polyester (PES)

- **Standard option**: Most common filter material
- **Use case**: General industrial dust, dry materials
- **Class certifiable**: M
- **Antistatic version**: Available for combustible dust applications

### PTFE (Teflon-coated polyester)

- **Premium option**: Anti-stick membrane
- **Use case**: Fine, sticky, or hard-to-release dust (cement, plaster, flour)
- **Class certifiable**: M
- **Advantage**: Easier filter cleaning, longer lifespan

### Nomex

- **Heat-resistant option**: Up to 250°C continuous, 300°C peak
- **Use case**: Hot materials, oven cleaning, welding residues
- **Application**: XM 20 OVEN and similar high-temperature applications

### Antistatic (ANT M)

- **Combustible dust option**: Carbon-impregnated polyester
- **Use case**: Dust with electrostatic ignition risk
- **Class certifiable**: M
- **Mandatory for**: ACD and DEX product variants

### PTFE Antistatic

- **Combination option**: Anti-stick + antistatic
- **Use case**: Fine combustible dust, food-grade ATEX applications
- **Class certifiable**: M
- **Application**: Bakeries, pharmaceutical processing

## Filter Cleaning Systems

### Manual

- **Mechanism**: Lever-operated shaker
- **Use case**: Occasional use, light dust load
- **Frequency**: Operator-initiated
- **Products**: Standard MINIBULL, XM 20 (without JC)

### Jet Clean (JC)

- **Mechanism**: Compressed-air pulse cleaning
- **Use case**: Continuous operation, fine/caking dust
- **Frequency**: Automatic during operation
- **Products**: XM 20 JC, BL 20 JC DEX, BL 45 JC DEX
- **Advantage**: No manual intervention, maintained airflow

### Automatic Backflush

- **Mechanism**: Reverse airflow cleaning
- **Use case**: Heavy-duty continuous operation
- **Frequency**: Cyclic, programmable
- **Products**: PUMA SP, ECOBULL with automatic systems
- **Advantage**: Highest cleaning efficiency

### Pneumatic Shaker

- **Mechanism**: Air-actuated mechanical shaking
- **Use case**: Heavy industrial applications
- **Frequency**: Automatic or manual trigger
- **Products**: Industrial DF series, central vacuum systems

## Important notes

### Filter clogging indicator

For Class M and H certified vacuums, EN 60335-2-69 requires a filter clogging indicator (acoustic or visual) that activates when airflow drops below 20 m/s. This is standard on all Depureco M and H rated products.

### Filter replacement frequency

Filter lifespan depends on:
- Material characteristics (sticky, fine, abrasive)
- Cleaning system (manual vs automatic)
- Operating hours
- Air load (m³/h)

For continuous-duty applications, plan filter replacement every 6-12 months. For occasional use, filters can last several years.

### Compliance verification

For regulated environments (pharmaceutical, food, healthcare), filter integrity testing may be required at installation and periodic intervals. Depureco provides certified test reports on request.
